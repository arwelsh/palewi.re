 Applications

A selection of my programming work, professional and open-source. Everything was a team effort. I tried to pitch in.

Mapping L.A.: Crime

With daily up­dates, read­ers can keep cur­rent on loc­al crime and com­pare crime levels across more than 200 neigh­bor­hoods. When crimes rise sharply, the sys­tem de­liv­ers a crime alert.

Map: Thomas Suh Lauder

Mapping L.A.: Neighborhoods

Maps and stat­ist­ics for more than 200 neigh­bor­hoods with­in L.A. County. Map­ping L.A. began in 2009 with pub­lic­a­tion of The Times’ map of 87 neigh­bor­hoods in the city of L.A., re­drawn with the help of read­ers.

California’s War Dead

The Times’ collection of stories about California servicemembers who died during the wars in Iraq and Afghanistan.

spreadsheets.latimes.com and Table Stacker

A framework for publishing interactive tables, and doing it on deadline. In use by The Times and open-sourced under the name Table Stacker.

Photo: Los Angeles Times

Hollywood Star Walk

The Times’ virtual tour puts readers on the streets of Hollywood to visit the nearly 2,400 stars on the Walk of Fame.

documents.latimes.com

The Times has pub­lished thou­sands of pages of public doc­uments, ranging from Ruben Sal­azar’s killing to the Bell scandal.

jQuery Geocodify

A jQuery plug-in that provides autocomplete for address searches.

DocumentCloud API for Python

A simple python wrapper for the DocumentCloud API.

L.A. Metro API for Python

A simple Python wrapper for the L.A. Metro’s API for bus stops, routes and vehicles.

AppEngine Template

Bootstrap a Google App Engine project with Django and other goodies.

Photo: placekitten.com

Django Greeking

Tools for printing filler text in your Django templates, a technique from the days of hot type known as greeking.
